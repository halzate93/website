Jaguar

A platformer that tells the tale of a kid that reunites with his wild side

The voice of the jaguar is a platformer for PC and Mac based on the original book with the same title, its a puzzle platformer that poses both skill and analytical challenges.

The game revolves around the ability the kid has to transform into a powerful jaguar, while still having to choose the right moments to do so according to the skills each character has.

My role for the project was tech lead and lead developer, I worked on the gameplay mechanics as well as building tools for the game design team while working together with a small team of developers.

Shock you

A fun and chaotic platforms fighting game developed at the GGJ-2017

Shock you started as a bare idea based in the theme for the Global Game Jam 2017, which was Waves. So we created a competitive multiplayer game with support for up to 4 players where they have to fight in order to survive while bouncing in unstable platforms and surviving other players attacks.

The game was developed through the game jam, which duration was 48 hours of non-sleep hard and fun work. We won the "Best Game" award in our location at UPB Medellin.

Meteorz

A retro space-shooter for arcade fans; defend yourself and your people!

A quick paced arcade shooter for retro game fans, Meteorz is set in a distant future where mankind’s survival is at peril. Protect the planets from space hazards using missiles, laser beams and over 24 other skills, while you collect enough energy to bring new hope for your people and build a new home.

Fast-paced mechanics, focused on arcade-lovers with a retro aesthetic. It is available on both iOS and Android with a full version and on Android with a free-to-play version with microtransactions and adds.

On this project I was involved on the core-mechanics development, artificial intelligence, in-game store, testing and prototyping. It was developed using agile methodologies.

Brainztorm

A powerful unity sdk to develop free-to-play mobile games

An sdk that includes common tools related to developing free-to-play games for mobile devices: daily missions, inbox, questing system, user data, dialogues, localisation, items, store, security, etc.

My role in the project was lead developer of the front-end team. I designed, developed and integrated complex systems that can be reused and extended easily. A great effort of automated testing was crucial for the success of the project.

Decubed

An educational game to teach programming to high-school students.

Decubed is a serious game (games that serve serious purposes) used as a supplementary tool to teach high school students the fundamentals of programming: instructions, conditionals, loops and functions. It was used by instructors on short courses and full-time high school teachers in Medellín-Colombia.

There's 2 modes: arcade and educational. On both modes the player has to solve a series of puzzles using different characters with powers such as emiting laser, floating, sliding, etc. On the educational mode the players coded the answer instead of using the characters directly.

It's opensource!, supported by an active research group and quite fun.

I was part of the game design and development teams.

Frankenbird

Interactive installation as a residency project for the Kamloops Art Gallery.

I had the opportunity to do a residency with the Kamloops Art Gallery to explore interactive art and other uses of new media. As part of an artist group we did site-specific installation on Kamloops downtown.

The project consisted of a looping background video with an animated character that reacted to people through a motion sensor. I had a projector, a couple of speakers and a Kinect sensor on the room to bring the piece to life.

People walked in and played with the piece.

I did the whole project on my own, including: video and sound editing and recording, basic animation, interaction and sensor programming.
