About me

I'm an interactive app developer based in Medellín-Colombia. I build interactive experiences, games, animated books, etc. I've done some research at the university on serious games, simulators and ways to use new media to solve problems. Also, I'm proficient on different image, sound and video editing software.

It's been a fun ride this far:

  • Spent two years as co-founder and developer at an indie game development studio (two published games).
  • Involved with research: three years as director of a videogame and virtual reality research group; we did research and worked to create a tight game-developer community in our city.
  • A year abroad finishing university, working on research and doing creative freelance projects.

I've worked hard on personal projects, worked both in the industry and academy. Also I have a wide interest on photography, film-making, games, and a bunch of weird stuff.

Meteorz

A retro space-shooter for arcade fans; defend yourself and your people!

A quick paced arcade shooter for retro game fans, Meteorz is set in a distant future where mankind’s survival is at peril. Protect the planets from space hazards using missiles, laser beams and over 24 other skills, while you collect enough energy to bring new hope for your people and build a new home.

It had fast-paced mechanics, focused on arcade-lovers with a retro aesthetic. It is available on both iOS and Android with a full version and on Android with a free-to-play version with microtransactions and adds.

On this project I was involved on the core-mechanics development, artificial intelligence, in-game store, testing and prototyping. It was developed using agile methodologies.

Monster Animation

Custom 2D sprite animation plugin for Unity3D.

In the days where Unity3D didn't natively support 2D games, we saw the need to get 2D animated sprites on it to take advantage of the robust engine they offered. So we created Monster Animation: a plugin downloadable from the asset store to create simple 2D animations and configuring them to the developer's needs.

It had an extensible API, could be used by scripting or using the convenient Unity3D editor. We deprecated it when Unity announced 2D support.

I worked on the core-library and went through the publishing and support process.

Decubed

An educational game to teach programming to high-school students.

Decubed is a serious game (games that serve serious purposes) used as a supplementary tool to teach high school students the fundamentals of programming: instructions, conditionals, loops and functions. It was used by instructors on short courses and full-time high school teachers in Medellín-Colombia.

There was 2 modes: arcade and educational. On both modes the player had to solve a series of puzzles using different characters with powers such as emiting laser, floating, sliding, etc. On the educational mode the players coded the answer instead of using the characters directly.

It's opensource, supported by an active research group and quite fun.

I was part of the game design and development team.

Frankenbird

Interactive installation as a residency project for the Kamloops Art Gallery.

I had the opportunity to do a residency with the Kamloops Art Gallery to explore interactive art and other uses of new media. As part of an artist group we did site-specific installation on Kamloops downtown.

The project consisted of a looping background video with an animated character that reacted to people through a motion sensor. I had a projector, a couple of speakers and a Kinect sensor on the room to bring the piece to life.

People walked in and played with the piece.

I did the whole project on my own, including: video and sound editing and recording, basic animation, interaction and sensor programming.

Crowd Control

Agent-based model to simulate crowd behaviour for policing purposes.

I developed an agent-based model of artificially intelligent entities that resembled the behaviour of human crowds, based on a psychological model called ESIM (Elaborated Social Identity Model).

It was used to create scenarios and get a better idea on how to handle them from a policing perspective. This project was presented on the Thompson Rivers University and Irving K. Barber undergraduate research conferences.

My role on this project was main researcher and developer.

Innova

Publicitary game for an innovation congress at EAFIT university.

We developed this game for the organizers of the 6th innovation congress at EAFIT University, players were introduced to the event through the game on social networks and competed for the chance to win a free ticket for the congress that showcased exceptional innovators of the country.

The game was a single level runner where the player had to jump and slide to avoid different obstacles combinations. The environment was inspired on the actual university.

On this project I performed as project manager, game developer and designer and backend developer.
